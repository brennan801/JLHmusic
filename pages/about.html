About Lopez Hunter Music

Music is amazing because it gives us a way to express ourselves and better understand the world around us. One of my favorite things about music is that it is interdisciplinary. That means that when we study music, we study lots of other things like physics, biology, math, art, foreign language, technology, and culture. Learning music strengthens students mentally, emotionally, and even physically.

Why join my studio? I love teaching and I am dedicated to helping my students progress. The flute is a versatile instrument that is found in lots of different genres: classical, pop, religious (you can play in church!), jazz, world music, and many others! As you join me for lessons, I will give you the tools to become a better musician who can perform frequently and confidently. About Julia Lopez Hunter

Julia Lopez Hunter recently graduated from Snow College with her Bachelor of Music with an Emphasis in Commercial Music. She began playing flute in sixth grade, and has been in love with music and music education ever since. Julia is trained primarily as a classical flute player, but she also loves delving into other instruments, genres, and fields of music. She especially enjoys learning about the violin, voice, jazz/commercial music, music technology, and business. She has had the great pleasure of studying under amazing private instructors such as Margie Halling, Elaine Jorgensen, Christina Castellanos, Susan Huff, and Kathy Hunter. She is incredibly grateful for the immense knowledge gained from these women and all her professors at Snow College.

Since learning flute, Julia has participated in groups such as wind bands, symphony orchestras, pit orchestras, chamber ensembles, commercial music ensembles, and choirs. She hopes to continue to perform throughout her entire life. She loves music education and is grateful for the two years she helped lead the Snow College chapter of CNAfME (Collegiate National Association for Music Education). In the past few years, she has fulfilled her passion for teaching and music as a physics tutor for Snow College, a beginning flute teacher at Ephraim Middle School, and a private lesson teacher. Having completed her degree, Julia is now pursuing her K-12 teaching license through Weber State University. She hopes to teach music in the public schools and maintain a studio of private flute students. In her personal life, she loves spending time with her friends and family, reconnecting with her heritage, attempting to cook with her husband, and learning about other cultures.
